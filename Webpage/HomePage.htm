At Home Work Outs How to Count Calories

Are you stuck in the house, feeling bored or wanting to start working towards being more healthy?

Is your answer maybe?

Many people are stuck in the house during the COVID-19 pandemic and may be starting to become extremely bored with their new sedentary lifestyle but just because you are stuck in the house does not mean that you cannot be physical or start making healthy lifestyle changes. This page contains information on things that you can do while you are stuck in the house that can make a change for the better.

Where To Start

The best place to start for making healthy changes while stuck in the house is to straighten up your living domicile. By doing this you are not only getting the blood flowing, but you are also allowing yourself to have room to think, grow and begin any of the next tasks.

Getting in the Kitchen

Now with your newly renovated household, you may have either worked up an appetite or just want to start working on eating healthier in order to feel better. Eating healthy is a good way to feel better and be creative in the kitchen. Foodnetwork.com is a great place to start for looking up healthy recipes to create in the kitchen. But if you are not too fond of cooking gourmet meals, a good rule of thumb for prepping meals is to make a third of a the plate some form of protein, a third a starchy carb, and a third filled with veggies. Getting Active

Just because you do not have a gym membership or it may be closed doesnt mean that you cannot get active in your own home. Doing yoga, sets of pushups, situps, or even going for a nice easy job are great ways of practice good physical fitness. Not only will you feel good but sometimes the results that you gain may surprise you. Below are a few good examples of these four activities.

  • Yoga Stretches
  • Performing Pushups
  • Performing Situps
  • Running

Rest

Whether you are stuck in the house due to COVID-19 or you have a normal daily routine, getting the proper amount of rest is key to living a healthy lifestyle. Most professionals recommend trying to get between 6-8 hours of uninterrupted sleep. During sleeping hours is when the body does most of its recovering from daily activities. It is also recommended to enforce a sleep schedule to optimize rest and recovery.
