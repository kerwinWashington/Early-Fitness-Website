The MURPH

Are you ready?

What is the MURPH?

The MURPH is an advanced cross fit workout that focuses on endurance as much as it does on being one of the most difficult workouts out there. This can be done at home, in a gym, or supplemented with the equipment available which only requires a pull up bar and a 20 pound vest if so desired.

Where it came from

The MURPH is a workout that is done in remembrance of LT Michael Murphy who was killed in action in Afghanistan on June 28th, 2005. This was a workout that he would constantly perform, originally, called “Body Armor”, which was renamed after him a few years later in reembrace of him. Hundreds of Americans will perform this workout on Memorial Day weekend as a testament to remember LT. Murphy and other countless Americans who have given their lives in the service of their country.

To perform the workout participants must perform:

1 mile run
100 pull ups
200 push ups
300 squats
1 mile run

Performing the workout

Some people choose to perform this workout while wearing a 20 pound vest, much like LT. Murphy did, and will also complete it for time. So far the records have been around 30 minutes by advanced athletes. Participants do not have to perform this workout for time, as some wish to test their endurance and physicality. Additionally, there are substitutions that can be done if the participant does not have to required equipment or fitness level. Instead of performing 100 pull ups, 400 sit ups can be substituted, 500 jumping jacks can be substituted for the 1 mile run, and if the participant cannot perform this high volume everything can be divided in half.
