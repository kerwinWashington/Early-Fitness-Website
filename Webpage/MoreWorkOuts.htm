This Page Contatins Workouts That Anyone Can Complete At Home

Which workout will you choose?

Push Ups

Pushups are one of the most common at home workouts that anyone can try. Even if you do not have enough upper body strength to perform regular pushups there are alternatives that can be performed. To begin, get down on the floor with your hands shoulder width apart, arms fully extended and your toes on the ground. From here lower your chest towards the ground until you touch and then push your chest back up with your arms. To view an example click here. For an alternative for those with less strength, instead of starting with your toes on the floor, start with your knees touching the floor to ease the stress on your upper body. To view an example click here.

Sit Ups

Sit ups are a great at home ab workout. To perform a sit up, start with your back on the floor and bend your knees until your heels are about 12-18 inches from your lower torso. Next cross your arms (or put your fingers behind your ears) and curl your head towards your knees where you should feel the contraction within your abs. To view an example click here.

Squats

Squats are a great leg work out that involves a lot of the muscles in the leg. Normally this is done in gym with various types of weighted equipment but they can also be turned into a body weight exercise. To start, stand straight up with feet shoulder width apart. From here lower your torso down bending your knees and flexing your hips until your upper legs are parallel to the floor or a little lower depending on flexibility. From here, return to the starting position. To view an example click here.

Burpees

Burpees are a great cardiovascular workout that involves numerous muscles in the body. To start, stand straight up in a well open area and then drop down to the floor until you are in the beginning of the push up position. From here jump back up to the beginning position and then perform a jump straight up into the air. To view an example click here.

Gallon of Water Curls

This workout only requires a gallon of water jug or two if you feel so inclined. The purpose of this workout is to act the same as a normal bicep curl but instead of using a dumbell you use a gallon of water. Start by grabbing the gallon jug with your arm by your side and simply curling your hand up until your hand is just above your elbow or nearly to your shoulder. By using a gallon jug, you can add or subtract water in order to vary the weight. To view an example click here.
